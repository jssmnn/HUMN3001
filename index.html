The Origins of Damper

In the morining, when you wake

before you leave for school

We all sit down for a meal

to keep us well-fuelled.

In the morning we eat breakfast, it's simply a must have.

So what do you eat when you eat your breakfast?

Do you eat cereal? Do you eat toast?

What's your favourite? What do you eat the most?

The reason I ask, is because, back in the day

Coles and Woolies weren’t around, so in order to stay

happy and full the folks of the past

had to make their breakfast entirely from scratch.

Across Australian history, there's a dish that still remains,

A bread that is still made today — do you know its name?

Well, it’s baked in hot coals, and tastes wonderful with honey.

Why! It’s called damper, and it is ever so yummy.

Baking bush damper is a tradition so ancient

That it was eaten by first people of this nation.

The first to ever hunt, farm and care for this land,

a culture that continues today, you must understand.

To you and me, this stone just look like a rock,

But when it was found it caused a great shock!

In 2015, Archaeologists dug deep in the ground

and there they found "the oldest known seed grinding tools in Australia."

Indigenous Australians have been baking for 60 000 years,

They would grind down grain until a flour appeared.

They would mix in some water, bake it in coals — a whole mob it would feed.

They cultivated plants and seed, tending to the land's needs.

Similar grinding stones have been found all over this nation,

they are ancient relics of the oldest continuous civilisation.

These stones hold secrets of humanities origins and survival

A culture that was forever changed after Captain Cooks' arrival.
